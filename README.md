# Banal

**AI That Belongs to People Who Have Nothing**

No account. No credit card. No install. No permission.

Just a link. Or a file on a USB. Or a folder you copied in ten minutes.

This is not another AI wrapper. This is an attempt to return something that was stolen.

---

## The Theft

For most of human history, the ability to think ahead, to plan, to argue your case — these were skills you developed or inherited.

Then came the machines that could do it better than almost any human.

The people who could pay got the best versions first. Everyone else got the waiting room, the rate limit, the message that their problem would have to wait until tomorrow — or until they found $20 a month they didn't have.

The models were trained on billions of words written by ordinary people. Our stories, our pain, our ways of surviving. Then the same systems that learned from us began charging us rent to use the patterns we helped create.

Banal exists because this arrangement is not inevitable.

---

## What Banal Actually Is

Banal is a single, static website with 9 high-leverage prompt templates written for the real situations poor and stressed people face:

- Turning caregiving gaps, illness, unemployment into cover letters that don't apologize
- Building learning plans using only free resources with 15 minutes a day
- Starting tiny ethical hustles with literally zero upfront money
- Writing factual letters to welfare offices, landlords, hospitals
- Decoding terrifying official letters into plain language + actions
- 5-minute grounding when you have nothing left
- Negotiating debt while keeping your dignity and mental health
- Bridging English and Japanese worlds with dignity
- Turning unpaid caregiving into STAR stories hiring managers recognize

It also contains a working chat interface connected to providers that don't require payment (Groq, Gemini free tier, Hugering Face), with smart routing and graceful errors.

Every conversation can be exported as a single, self-contained HTML file that will open anywhere, forever.

The entire project is one folder. A person on a library computer can understand it in an afternoon and host their own copy by dinner.

## Honest Limitations

We will not lie to you.

**"Free" means different things:**
- `no-login` — Open in any browser, no account, no payment
- `public-api` — No key needed, just call the URL
- `open-source` — Free to use, may need local setup
- `free-tier` — Free account required, some limits apply

**AI tools are not magic:** They can hallucinate, give wrong answers, or refuse legitimate requests. Always verify important information.

**Rate limits are real:** Free tiers exist to share resources. When you hit a limit, wait or try another tool.

**Privacy matters:** Do not treat any hosted AI as confidential legal, medical, or financial counsel. Your prompts may be logged.

**Tools change:** Free tiers can be reduced or removed. Always test yourself.
